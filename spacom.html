SPACOM-1

Spacom-1 ~ refers to the first Model of our Solar Powered Air Conditioner Operated Walk-in Cold Room. A spacom is a 2.5m long, 2.3m wide and 2.6m high walk-in cold room with a total volume of 14.95 cubic meters that was constructed using Interlocking Stabilised Soil Bricks (ISSB) and insulated with AES R~8 HVAC Duct wrap insulator enabling it to be airtight and achieve the insulation value of R24. Spacom is operated by an 18000 BTU air conditioner unit powered by either electricity or solar. Spacom is designed to achieve temperatures ranging from 10 to 3 degrees Celsius (50 to 37 degrees Fahrenheit).

What makes Spacoms unique?

Using an air conditioner unit to operate Spacoms instead of the traditional separate condenser, compressor, and evaporator units in conventional cold storage rooms lowers the energy used per unit cooling time by 50% and this has two major advantages;

  • Firstly, using AC significantly lowers the operating costs of Spacom, reducing electricity bills if used on the grid and (or) eliminating the entire electricity bill if it's connected to solar panels. This makes it feasible for small-scale farms and SMEs to afford cold storage as a food preservation method while ensuring more return on investment.
  • Secondly, ACs use small quantities of coolant compared to the cooling system of the conventional cold storage room; thus, fewer emissions of Hydrofluorocarbons (HFC) should leakages occur. Additionally, introducing HFCs as new-generation coolants in ACs instead of Chlorofluorocarbons (CFCs) reduced the occurrences of leakages by 60%.
Why should you use Spacoms?

The temperature inside the Spacom can be adjusted using a thermostat to suit the recommended temperature for storing particular food products. Spacom is a product of design thinking with local farmers and Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs) in mind. Thus, it is constructed from cheap and readily available materials modified to achieve the recommended insulation R-Value for cold storage. This enables us to supply Spacoms at relatively low prices, just a fraction of the cost of constructing a conventional Cold Storage Room of similar capacity and efficiency.
