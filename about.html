Modern Agriculture Solutions

Agrosage-Tek Uganda Limited (ATUL) is a social enterprise established in 2021 to harness young people's energy, creativity, and willpower to eradicate poverty and mitigate hunger in East Africa by reducing post-harvest. Our mission is to contribute towards achieving zero hunger (SDG 2) in Sub-Saharan Africa, using affordable, effective, innovative, reliable and environmentally friendly food production and preservation techniques while creating employment opportunities for young people and mitigating poverty among rural farmers. Our vision is to become the leading provider of affordable, dependable, effective, and environmentally friendly food production and preservation technology in Uganda, East Africa, and Africa. ATUL is a product of five years of co-foundersâ€™ experience working to solve the hunger problem in Uganda through post-harvest loss reduction.

ATUL was perceived from our burning desire to solve the several challenges we encountered while using heat-based dehydration methods to preserve food in the previous venture. Through design thinking, we observed that farmers needed food preservation technology that could preserve relatively large amounts of food, yet easy and safe to operate, could bring significant returns on investment and could be scaled without damaging the environment. The global struggle to reduce CO2 emissions and mitigate climate change renders ineffective any food preservation technology that relies on burning organic as a source of energy. ATUL was established to provide farmers with an alternative food preservation technology that is affordable, effective, safe, reliable, and environmentally friendly and will enable them to eradicate poverty and mitigate hunger. Our first food preservation project is the Solar-powered air conditioner-operated cold room (Spacom-1). It can preserve perishable food such as fish, meat, fruits, vegetables, and dairy products at temperatures as low as three degrees Celsius. It is suitable for use by small and larger scale farmers, market vendors, schools, refugee camps, government projects, non-government organizations and health centres in remote areas
